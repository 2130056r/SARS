What Is Systematic Review?

A systematic review is a type of literature review that collects and critically analyzes multiple research studies or papers. This is accomplished through deeming each piece of data as relevant or not relevant to it's chosen subject. Once completed the left over information is considered completely worthy of time and effort. A review of existing studies is often quicker and cheaper than embarking on a new study. Why They Are Needed.

Systematic reviews of documentation and information is incredibly valuable. With the completion of a systematic review the knowledge that has passed the test of being considered valuable information by the reviewers is now deemed relevant to the topic it is based on. With this tedious amount of reviewing the small amount that gets through is considered to be true, fair and worth while information all else is now deemed, basically, a waste of time and this saves people time when trying to research and in some cases (especially medical) time is the most valuable resource available.
